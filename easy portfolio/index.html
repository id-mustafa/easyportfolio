About

Hello world! I’m an aspiring software engineer and database systems aficionado, currently enriching my knowledge base at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill in the Computer Science department. My academic focus lies in the intricate world of software engineering and database systems, where I’m dedicated to understanding the underpinnings of robust tech infrastructures and the efficient handling of data.

Parallel to my studies, I am actively engaged in data visualization and IT roles at UNC. Here, I apply my growing knowledge to real-world problems, crafting visual narratives from complex data as a Data Visualization Engineer and fortifying the backbone of our digital environment as an IT Technician. These roles have not only been integral to my practical learning but also to the impact I’ve been able to make within the academic community.

In addition to my on-campus engagement, I have ventured into the entrepreneurial realm as the founder of FetchTek, a company that embodies my passion for the intersection of technology and commerce. FetchTek has been a phenomenal platform for me to exercise and expand my skills in finance, project management, and operational strategy, all while navigating the fast-paced world of electronics trade.

My technical journey, which began with self-taught programming in 2019, has evolved into a multifaceted career path where education, practical experience, and entrepreneurial ventures intersect. Through FetchTek, I have not only navigated the challenges of business management but have also been at the helm of creating a customer-centric digital marketplace, leveraging my understanding of software and database systems to craft an efficient and secure platform for electronics trade.

As a perpetual learner and an innovator, I am on a quest to blend the theoretical frameworks from my education with the hands-on experience from my work to leave a lasting imprint on the tech industry. I am keen on collaborating with visionaries and organizations that are committed to using technology as a force for positive change and advancement.

Let's build the future together!

Computer Organization

This class teaches the hardware portion of how a computer functions. It taught me how to design circuits with logic gates, registers, and ALUs (Arithmetic Logic Units). I also learned how to write MIPS assembly programs, how to minimize transistor count, and how to decipher timing graphs with propagation delays.

Files and Databases

A course on the relational database management system (RDBMS), data manipulation language (DML), and data definition language (DDL). interests

This page is meant to be a place where I can express some of the innovative thoughts I have and what interests me

  1. Idea: Building a multinational tech conglomerate that solves problems to different areas of tech from server hosting and cloud infrastructure to Cybersecurity tools to Computer Hardware to Data Solutions and visualization AI. I'm interested in this because in order to truly be a successful company and entrepreneur, you would need to add value across the board in multiple sectors.
  2. Idea: What if a company's data needs were to be automated? Working as a Data Visualization Engineer right now for UNC, I've come to realize how integral data is to every business decision that the school makes. Oftentimes, my work consists of getting a request from an admin that says "We would like to find how many students participated in X organization during Y year(s)." and I feel like maybe there is a way to automate these requests to be handled by a data-centric AI.
  3. Idea: If you're anything like me, you grew up on Sci-Fi shows that showcased a series of holograms as a part of their utopia. What if these holograms don't have to be fiction anymore? What if we can find a way to bring these to life by distorting the way photons collide with one another to create an exact image of what we'd like to see. This is how projectors work but what if we can find a way to suspend photons in place to create this seemingly floating image?
  4. Idea: Ever heard stories about how machine learning is so saturated and how the LLM's take so long to train? Part of the imminent future is finding a way to speed up the learning of these models so that we can train AI in seconds rather than months. We need to drastically include every possible outcome and train the AI model in a quantum capacity. I'd like to join the race to combine AI and Quantum mechanics fundamentals to speed up the time it takes to train an AI.
  5. Idea: Battery technology are slowly going out of fashion and their usability is limited as their capacities degrade over time. The idea is to utilize an energy source as potent as uranium without its radioactive risks invites consideration of advanced nuclear technologies, such as fusion energy or the use of radioisotope thermoelectric generators (RTGs) in a more controlled manner. Fusion energy, in particular, remains the holy grail of energy research, offering the promise of abundant, clean energy from reactions similar to those powering the sun. However, practical, controlled fusion for use in portable devices remains a formidable technical challenge, requiring significant breakthroughs in containment and net energy production.
  6. Idea: One of my big dreams is to aid in the space exploration for a new world beyond our own. As I grew up reading more and more on NASA's acheivement and research, I noticed that there have been planets like ours discovered thousands of light years away. Future propulsion technologies such as nuclear thermal propulsion (NTP), nuclear electric propulsion (NEP), and even theoretical concepts like antimatter engines, present feasible pathways to reduce travel time markedly. For instance, NTP, utilizing nuclear reactions as a heat source to expel propellant at high speeds, offers a specific impulse several times greater than conventional chemical rockets. This could potentially cut down the travel time to Mars by half, setting a precedent for more ambitious interstellar voyages.
  7. Idea: Some black holes, particularly supermassive black holes at the centers of galaxies, emit powerful jets of electromagnetic radiation and subatomic particles at nearly the speed of light. These jets are believed to be generated by the magnetic fields around the accretion disks of black holes, though the precise mechanism is still a topic of research. Theoretically, these relativistic jets could be harnessed as cosmic-scale particle accelerators, potentially revealing new physics that are inaccessible to Earth-bound particle colliders. The interaction of these jets with the interstellar medium could provide valuable insights into high-energy physics and the behavior of matter under extreme conditions. What if there was a way we can harness this energy and fundamentally reshape the physics of our world today?
  8. Idea: Current recycling efforts often focus on straightforward reuse or rudimentary processing of materials such as plastics, metals, and paper. However, these processes sometimes overlook the energy-intensive nature of recycling or the degradation in quality of recycled materials. Advanced repurposing seeks to transform waste into higher-value applications, thereby offering a dual benefit: reducing environmental impact and generating greater economic value from what was previously considered waste.
  9. Idea: In the future, I would like to be involved in launching my very own real estate private equity firm. Having gotten my real estate license as soon as I turned 18 years old, I joined the cutthroat world of real estate, I found that real estate development had a significant impact on several key factors of human geography and socio-economic developent. My focus is to primarily introduce real estate in areas that are underdeveloped and have a struggling economic status. Ultimately reshaping cities to be desirable for companies that attract talent in the area and beyond, essentially creating the next generation of wall street, silicon valley, and salt lake city.
  10. Idea: Not all my ambitions have to be too entrepreneurial or company-driven. Another goal of mine is to give back to disadvantaged and struggling communities in countries around the world. That doesn't mean that I would like to dump a pool of money in one area, that means I would like to introduce infrastructure for cities and villages that have no infrastructure from their respective governments. It is as the old adage goes, give a man a fish and you will only feed him for a day, but if you teach the man how to fish, then you will feed him for life. I'd like to not only create infrastructure but also fund and create schools that support that infrastructure's continued development through social engineering.
